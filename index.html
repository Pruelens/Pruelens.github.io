Postdoctoral Researcher at the laboratory of Genetics, Wageningen University and Research in the Netherlands 

I'm an evolutionary biologist with a general interest in understanding the factors that influence the tempo and mode of gene evolution. In layman's terms, what is possible evolutionary (genotype-phenotype map) and what are the actual accesible evolutionary paths. And if there is a discrepancy between the two, what are the factors affecting this difference. To do this, I use a combination of experimental, phylogenetic and modelling approaches. As I am interested in the universal drivers of evolution, I prefer to work on a wide range of different organisms and mechanisms.

 I am always happy to talk about evolution, research in general, or science education. Outside of the lab, I enjoy tinkering with computers, gardening and spending time with our two children. Whether looking for a research collaborator or you would like to know more about my research, do not hesitate to reach out via email.
